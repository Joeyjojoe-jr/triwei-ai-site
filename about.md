TriWei AI is a noncommercial source-linking and research website for people trying to understand artificial intelligence without confusing repetition, promotion, or machine-generated prose with verified knowledge.

## What readers can use now

TriWei currently publishes:

- rotating topic folders that link to direct original sources;
- source metadata such as title, author, publisher, date, identifier, and source type;
- source-dated specifications or measurements with inspectable citations;
- a Research Lineage Library with direct arXiv records and provisional chronological reading paths;
- hardware, industry, and ethics source registers; and
- transparent descriptions of automated collection and AI-assisted source-discovery methods and their limitations.

Original TriWei analysis may be published only after human research, human authorship, source review, attribution review, and approval.

## Features being rebuilt

Some earlier visualizations and explanatory features were useful ideas but did not yet satisfy the site's authorship, evidence, and attribution requirements. They are not simply erased or presented as finished. The public [Workbench & Roadmap](/workbench/) records:

- what remains useful and available now;
- what the intended feature should eventually provide;
- why its previous implementation is not being published;
- the acceptance gate required before it can return; and
- which methods TriWei has retired permanently.

## What TriWei does not present as knowledge

TriWei does not publish AI-written synopses of third-party articles or papers that TriWei did not independently research and human-author. It also does not present:

- headline frequency as proof of importance or truth;
- automated tags as findings of wrongdoing, legality, safety, or merit;
- provider claims as independent adjudication;
- vendor specifications as independent benchmarks;
- chronological reading order as proof of influence, causation, priority, or importance;
- source inclusion as endorsement; or
- a collected sample as a complete account of the field.

When a direct original source cannot be identified, TriWei prefers an incomplete folder or visible source-gap notice over an intermediary link, copied text, guessed attribution, or generated substitute.

## How to use the site

Use the rotating folders on the [homepage](/) to open original sources and navigate into narrower topic folders. Use the [Research Lineage Library](/signals/) to locate original scholarly records. Use [AI Hardware](/hardware/), [Industry Data](/industry/), and [Ethics Sources](/ethics/) for source-dated information. Use the [Workbench](/workbench/) to see which richer features are being rebuilt and exactly what must be completed before publication.

The [Sources & Method](/sources/) page contains the binding publication contract. The [Corrections & Revisions](/corrections/) page records material changes and explains how to report a problem.

## Limits

TriWei is curated, incomplete, and subject to error. It is not a substitute for reading the original source, consulting a qualified professional, or conducting independent research. It does not provide legal, medical, financial, safety, procurement, or investment advice.
