<section class="mall-page-hero mall-panel">
  <p class="mall-kicker">Project boundaries</p>
  <h1>Methodology and boundaries</h1>
  <p class="mall-lead">TriWei AI is an independent educational and experimental project. The site explains systems and displays technical experiments; it does not provide personal, political, legal, or investment commentary.</p>
</section>

<section class="mall-policy-grid">
  <article class="mall-panel"><h2>Educational scope</h2><p>Exhibits simplify individual concepts so visitors can inspect a mechanism or tradeoff. Simplification is disclosed and should not be treated as a full model specification.</p></article>
  <article class="mall-panel"><h2>No professional advice</h2><p>Nothing on the site should be treated as legal, financial, medical, investment, or other professional advice for a specific person or situation.</p></article>
  <article class="mall-panel"><h2>No endorsement by inclusion</h2><p>External links, machine-collected headlines, and automated labels are inputs to an experiment. Their inclusion does not indicate endorsement, accuracy, importance, or agreement.</p></article>
  <article class="mall-panel"><h2>Privacy-minimal design</h2><p>The site avoids accounts, forms, advertising networks, tracking pixels, and production AI APIs. Browser-local preferences are not transmitted by the site's code.</p></article>
  <article class="mall-panel"><h2>Provenance</h2><p>Future exhibits that use outside research, images, or datasets should identify the source, retrieval date, permitted use, and any transformation performed.</p></article>
  <article class="mall-panel"><h2>Correction standard</h2><p>Known errors should be corrected in the repository with a visible change history. Uncertainty should be stated rather than replaced by confident guesses.</p></article>
</section>

<section class="mall-panel mall-route-note">
  <h2>Automated news collection</h2>
  <p>The legacy news collector uses broad search queries and keyword rules. Those techniques can produce false matches. The system is therefore presented only as an Observatory instrument and is not used to define the site's public identity.</p>
</section>
